  Business Practices
Deadlines

Like many other places, Japan strictly values deadlines and expects you to be on time with completing your work, therefore, it is important to follow deadlines as not doing so can harm the company, affect the people's impression of you, and disrespect them as well.
 
Don't follow a hard approach

The Japanese business culture believes in taking a relatively calm and gentle approach towards doing business. Instead of being hard-selling and confrontational, try to be calm when you are pitching a new business model or idea. The Japanese do things by consensus and pushing them for deadlines can be detrimental to business, especially considering how strictly deadlines are respected, and trying to speed up the business process may seem disrespectful.
 
Punctuality

Similar to deadlines, punctuality is also greatly valued in Japanese business culture. Everyone is expected to be on time for business meetings and appointments. It is advisable to try to arrive 5 minutes early for such meetings, however, if you are late for such a meeting, you should inform the people in a polite and apologetic manner.
 
Decision making

Unlike a lot of western cultures, Japanese people make business decisions by 'consensus', which means a general agreement by the people in the organization instead of strict rules. The Japanese debate a proposed decision throughout the organization until there is agreement on it.
